Rockets

Prometheus Rocket

The prometheus rocket was developed alongside the blip flight computer to use active thrust vector control to control itself. To learn more about thrust vector control, Click here. Prometheus is for all extensive purposes, nearly ready for flight, only being delayedd by the blip flight computer. Blip takes in data from 2 sensors, and calculates the optimal motor angle to maintain stability. Then, blip sends that data to prometheus, which adjusts to servos the reorient the motor. It flies on an estes F15-0 rocket motor, and rescends on two parachutes. It flies to about 200 meters, and is built of two 3 inch body tube sections. The nose cone, as well as several parts around the rocket are 3d printed on an ender 3 3d printer.
Status: in development
In service: n/a
Motor: Estes F15-0
Parachutes: two
Diameter: 76mm
Length: 1 meter


Pegasus I Rocket

Pegasus was Gallantz Aerospace's first rocket, and its goal was to develop manufacturing and design capability for Gallantz Aerospace. It has flown twice currently, with 2 flights planned before it is retired and replaced with the Pegasus II. It is controlled with 3d printed fins, and reaches and apogee of about 250 meters
Status: in use
In service: nov 2020-current
Motor: Estes D12-7
Parachutes: 1
Diameter: 34mm
Length: 1 meter
